1. Vehicle Model

a. Contains three components

i. Manufacturer - ex. MARUTI

ii. Model - ex. SWIFT

iii. Variant - ex. 1.2 VDI (1248cc)(D)

2. Registered RTO

a. RTO = Regional Transport Office

b. State —> District —-> RTO

c. Multiple RTOs under a District

d. Format: State Abbreviations + 2 digit Numbers

Ex. HR-26, Here 26 represent Gurugram

RJ-14, Here 14 represents Jaipur

DL-01, Here 01 represents North Delhi

2. Registered RTO - Special case of Odisha

a. Original name of state: Orissa

b. So RTO abbreviation was: OR

c. “Orissa” renamed to “Odisha” in 2011

d. RTO abbreviation changed to : OD

3. Purchase Date

a. On the date you purchased vehicle

3. Previous Insurer

4. IDV (Insured Declared Value)

a. The maximum sum assured fixed by the insurer that is offered

in case of theft or total loss of the vehicle

b. Will it be higher value than current value of vehicle - No

c. Will it be lower value than current value of vehicle - No

d. It is current value of the vehicle - Yes

e. Higher value of vehicle = Higher IDV = Higher Premium

(OD Premium - directly proportional to - IDV)

a. As the vehicle gets old = IDV decrease

4. IDV (Insured Declared Value)

a. Fixed by insurer

b. Generally insurer gives a default idv

c. And a range of deviation, let say +- 20%

Ex. HDFC’s default IDV for a vehicle is : 4 lakhs

And a deviation of +-20%

So Min IDV = 4 lakh * 0.80 = 3 lakh 20 hazar

Max IDV = 4 lakh * 1.20 = 4 lakh 80 hazar

4. IDV (Insured Declared Value)

So you can take IDV in between of: 3.20 lakh to 4.80 lakh

So you will get lowest premium @ 4.80 lakh - ?

For a 2019 purchased vehicle, default IDV is : A

For the same vehicle 2020, default IDV will be: A+x

5. Passenger Cover

a. To secure the passengers in the vehicle

b. Component on TP Premium

c. 4 W: 50 Rs Premium for 1 seat with coverage of Rs. 1 Lakh

6. PA Owner Driver Cover

a. To secure driver who is owner of the vehicle

b. Component on TP Premium

c. 4 W: 330 Rs.

7. Paid Driver Cover

a. To secure driver who is paid or hired

b. 50 Rs. Premium for 4 W

7. TPPD Restricted

a. Component of TP Premium

7. NCB (No claim Bonus)

a. A reward given by an insurance company to an insured for not

raising any claim request during a policy year

b. For example your vehicle engine get damaged but you did not

claim

c. Range between 20-50%

d. 0, 20, 25, 35, 45, 50

e. It’s value get subtracted from OD Premium
